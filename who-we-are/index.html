Who we are

Hi, I'm Torin — a filmmaker, fully in.

I'm Torin — a wedding filmmaker based in Northwestern Ontario. I picked up a camera because I couldn't stand the thought of a day this big living on in a handful of blurry phone clips. There's a difference between remembering a wedding and getting to feel it again, and chasing that difference is what got me hooked.

Over the past six years I've filmed more than a hundred weddings — backyard ceremonies, cliffside vows, all-night dance floors — and somewhere along the way it stopped feeling like a job. I still get the nerves before a first look. I still tear up at the speeches. After all this time I genuinely, stubbornly love what I do, and I think that shows up on screen.

The moments I'm really after are the ones nobody asks for: the quiet exhale before the doors open, a dad wiping his eyes when he thinks no one's looking, the laugh that catches you off guard. The day moves fast, and most of the best parts happen in the spaces between. That's where I point the camera.

When you hire me, you get me — from the first email to the final frame. Nothing outsourced, nothing pre-packaged, no rotating crew you've never met. We'll talk through your vision and what matters most to you long before the day arrives, and then I'll shoot quietly and stay out of the way, so what ends up on screen is your wedding as it actually happened — not a version posed for the camera.

Thunder Bay is home, but a good story will take you anywhere, and I'll happily go. Wherever your day is, my goal is the same: a film that feels unmistakably like yours.

It's real. It's all in. And it lasts a lot longer than the flowers.

A second set of hands

When the day calls for two.

For bigger days — or any time a second angle makes the story better — I bring in Cassandra Carangi, owner of Captured by Cass. She's the first person I call, and not just because she's talented behind a camera (though she absolutely is).

Over years of shooting side by side we've built the kind of shorthand you can't fake — a glance across the room is enough to know who's catching what. Somewhere between the early call times and the late dance floors, the working relationship turned into a real friendship, with the work always at the centre of it.

What that means for you: two people who genuinely like working together, anticipate each other, and quietly cover the whole day from angles a single shooter never could.

Let's make something timeless.
